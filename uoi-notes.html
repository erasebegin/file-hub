Manga Research

Manga means ‘comic’ in Japanese. In Chinese 漫 means something that is done at will, that is improvised (随意), while 画 means to draw. So manga has always been quite spontaneous in nature. Drawings are usually quite simple, but very expressive.

Manga is said to have originated in Japan from scrolls dating back to the 12th century, and it is believed they represent the basis for the right-to-left reading style.

(image/jpeg)
Inside Katsushika Hokusai’s Manga books, said to be among the first examples of the comics form in history.

Among the first and to date most successful manga artists, there wereOsamu Tezuka[3], who created the famousAstro Boy, as well asMachiko Hasegawa, with hisSazae-san. Tezuka, known as the God of Manga and Godfather of Anime, invented the distinctive large eyes prominent in both fields.

manga was strongly shaped by US cultural influences. It is said that the large, expressive eyes characteristic of modern manga came from American classics such as Betty Boop and Disney cartoons.

Plant relationship with animals

  • food
    • herbivores eat plants
    • many species of insect or worm eat plants and are then consumed by lager animals
    • a fundamental part of the natural food process
  • oxygen
    • animals breathe oxygen and exhale carbon dioxide, plants to the opposite, making plant life vital to the continued survival of animal life
  • water
    • some dessert animals might survive on the water stored in plants such as cactuses
  • shelter
    • several species of monkey live in trees or spend a majority of their time in trees in order to avoid predators
    • birds also live in trees to avoid being eaten, and to give a vantage point to spot prey
    • squirrels make use of trees to store food
  • pollination and seed dispersal
    • bees, bats, birds and other animals help plants to spread pollen and seeds in exchange for nectar, water or other nutrients

Unit 6 - Simple Machines

Imagining a world without simple machines

  • Lesson 1
    • T gives a couple examples of the things in the classroom that are simple machines or that simple machines have been used to be make.
    • Use this to introduce Ss to the idea of a world without simple machines
    • Ss first discuss in groups what they are going to focus on. Each member of a group must choose 1 thing in the classroom to talk about.
    • They must include:
      • Why this is a simple machine or why a simple machine is required to make this thing.
      • how this would change if there were no simple machines
      • Is there an alternative?
      • How would the things we do in the classroom change if we did not have this thing?
    • Ss are first allowed a few minutes to talk in groups to decide what each person is going to talk about.
    • Ss report back and say what each member of their group will be discussing. Each person in a group must talk about something different.
    • Ss then, on their own, make notes in their book about their chosen object following the requirements above.
    • Groups present to class. Each person only has to briefly and simply outline their thoughts.
